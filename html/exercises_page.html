Learn to Protect Yourself Against a Phishing Email

How to Dectect a Phishing Email

1) After you open an email, hover your mouse over what could be a link (this includes regular blue-highlighted links and images).
A link will appear on the bottom left corner of your window. If it appears suspicious, don't click it because it is probably a phishing email.

2) Spelling mistakes.

3) Vague salutation such as "Dear Customer".

4) Urgernt and threatening language

5) Suspicious signature. For example, signature has an email address with a suspicious domain.

Still Not Sure If It's a Phiting Email?

Don't give personal information.

For example, if you get an email from your bank saying that you need to change your password. Don't click on any links on the email. Instead, open a new window, access your online bank account the same way you always do, and then change your passoword.

Another example, if you get an email saying that you won something, then see who the email is coming from. Next, google that person/company. If not much information comes up, then it is probably a scam.

You Think You Got a Phishing Email, What's Next?

If you are a student that lives on campus and is using the school's WiFi, then your school should have a system to report phishing emails.

You can also report phishing and learn more about it at https://www.us-cert.gov/report-phishing

Learn to Make a Strong and Memorable Password

Step One

Think of a memorable sentence

Example

The Flying Squirrel Is A Cute Animal


Step Two

Take the letter from the beginning of each word

Example

The Flying Squirrel Is A Cute Animal

Result: tfsiaca


Step Three

Take your favorite word(s) from the sentence and change it's correspoting letter(s) to Capital Letter(s)

Example

The Flying Squirrel Is A Cute Animal

Result: tFSiaca


Step Four

Add numbers and symbols.
Here I add $4 after my favorite words (Flying Squirrel) to make it easier to rember where the number and symbol goes. The password will be more secure if the number and symbols are in the middle of the password and not at the end or beginning.

Example

The Flying Squirrel $4 Is A Cute Animal

Result: tFS$4iaca

Final Password: tFS$4iaca

Learn to Protect Yourself Against a Phishing Email

How to Dectect a Phishing Email

  1. After you open an email, hover your mouse over what could be a link (this includes regular blue-highlighted links and images).
    A link will appear either by your cursor or on the bottom left corner of your window. If it appears suspicious, don't click it because it is probably a phishing email.

  2. Spelling mistakes or grammatical errors

  3. Vague salutation such as "Dear Customer"

  4. Urgernt and threatening language

  5. Suspicious Sender. For example, signature has a suspicious email address, or the reply-to address is suspicious


You Got a Phishing Email, What to Do Now?

Do not click on any of the links

If you are a student that lives on campus and is using the school's WiFi, then your school should have a system to report phishing emails.

You can also report phishing and learn more about it at https://www.us-cert.gov/report-phishing


Still Not Sure If It's a Phishing Email?

Don't give personal information.


Do your research before doing anything drastic such as clicking on any links or downloading any files.


Google can be your friend. If you get an email from an organization or individual, lets say IFTTT, you should not click on any links on the email or reply with any personal information. Instead go to Google or open an new window and go to IFTTT's official website and find out if you need to do anything with your account.

Other Forms of Scams

Pharming

When you type a URL for a certain website, an attacker can redirect you to a malicious website even if the user enters the correct website name.

To protect against most pharming attacks, you should only enter your credentails to HTTPS protected websties

Social Engineering Attack

Scenerio 1) Email.

An Attacker may hack one of your friends emails or social media accounts. Then, the attacker sends an email to every freind. When you open the email, you see it's from your friend so you click on the licks inside and suddently something downloads to your computer without your permission. This may be a virus. Most successful phishing and malware attacks occur because the user CLICKS something without thinking twice.


Scenerio 2) Telephone

If you get a call an organization or individual asking for help, telling you that you need to make a change to your account, or saying your won something, it is probably a scam. Do your research before giving any information away.

Learn to Make a Strong and Memorable Password

Step One

Think of a memorable sentence

Example

The Flying Squirrel Is A Furry Animal

Step Two

Take the letter from the beginning of each word

Example

The Flying Squirrel Is A Furry Animal

Result: tfsiafa


Step Three

Take your favorite word(s) from the sentence and change it's correspoting letter(s) to Capital Letter(s)

Example

The Flying Squirrel Is A Furry Animal

Result: tFSiafa


Step Four

Add numbers and symbols.

Here I add $4 after my favorite words (Flying Squirrel) to make it easier to rember where the number and symbol goes.

The password will be more secure if the number and symbols are in the middle of the password and not at the end or beginning.

Remember to make your password long. The longer it is, the more secure it is.

Example

The Flying Squirrel $4 Is A Furry Animal

Result: tFS$4iafa

Final Password: tFS$4iafa

Tips

1) Use Password Managers

2) Don't use the same password twice
